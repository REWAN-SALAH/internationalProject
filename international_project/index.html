Gender Inequality and Feminism in Turkey

Gender inequality remains a pressing issue in Turkey. Women face systemic challenges in accessing equal rights, including discrimination in workplaces, violence at home, and societal barriers that hinder their opportunities.

Despite constitutional reforms promising equality, the patriarchal culture often prevents their implementation. Feminist movements in Turkey have played a pivotal role in addressing issues such as domestic violence, child marriages, and wage inequality. Their activism continues to highlight injustices and push for systemic changes in law and practice.

As feminist groups rally for their rights, they emphasize the importance of education, legal reforms, and international cooperation to promote equality. Their fight is not just for women's rights but for a fairer, inclusive society.
